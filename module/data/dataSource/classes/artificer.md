**Maximum Hit Points (HP) gained per Rank:** 7

**Maximum Stamina (ST) gained per Rank:** 7

# **Arcane Battlecasting**

	As an Artificer, you are able to prepare and cast spells from the Arcane spell list:

* **Spells Prepared.** At 1st Rank, you may prepare 4 spells for this Class at the end of each long rest. You may cast those prepared spells using this feature.

* **Class Circle Cap.** Each spell you prepare may have a maximum baseline Circle of 1st Circle.

* **Heightening.** When you cast spells using this feature, you may heighten spells up to your Class Circle Cap.

* **Spell Points.** At 1st Rank, you gain 2 Spell Points. When you cast spells using this feature, you must expend Spell Points equal to the Circle of the spell. Spell Points are regained at the end of each long rest.

* **Spellcasting Modifier.** Your spellcasting modifier when casting spells using this feature is INT.

  * **Your Spell Attack Bonus** \= your INT \+ your Tier Bonus

  * **Your Spell Save DC** \= 8 \+ your INT \+ your Tier Bonus

  * **Spellcasting Checks** made with spells prepared for this class use your INT.

* **Signature Spell.** Your designated signature spell may be one of the 1st Circle or higher spells prepared for this feature.

* **Higher Ranks.** At higher Ranks, the spells prepared, Spell Points, and Class Circle Cap all improve as described in the Class Overview Table.

# **Artificer Field**

	"*What you would consider unstable experimentation is what I call unlocking its true potential."*

At 1st Rank, you choose an Artificer Field to study as you invent new magical creations. You gain features from this chosen Artificer Field at 1st, 4th, and 8th Rank. Your Artificer Field will *specialize* you for creating certain types of items:

* **Consumable.** Artificer Fields *specialized in consumables* grant new stock recipes and modify existing stock recipes.
* **Equipment.** Artificer Fields *specialized in equipment* create, upgrade, and modify armaments, wearables, or other equipment.

## **Overload Die**

	Some Artificer features use your *overload die*, which starts at 1d10. At higher Ranks, your overload die improves as described in the Class Overview Table.

# **Innovation**

	*You begin tinkering with arcane alchemy, complex machinery, and quantised magic.*

At 1st Rank, you have a number of *innovation points (IP)* equal to your Artificer Rank. At the end of each long rest, you may freely *assign* your IP using your Artificer features, including those granted by your Artificer Field. Benefits gained from IP persist while the IP is assigned to them. As a free action, you may also *unassign* some or all of your IP, which then become available to assign again at the end of your next long rest.

# **Improvise I**

	*Your greatest strength is your mind, and your weapon is your slapdash collection of Arcane doodads.*

	At 2nd Rank, at the end of each rest, you may create a Common gadget of your choosing that is *improvised*. An improvised gadget is destroyed at the end of your next rest.

# **Resourceful Assembly**

	*Doing more with less.*

	At 2nd Rank, you gain the following benefits:

* **Ingenuity.** When you roll your resource die for a value check to start a crafting project, you may replace the outcome with your Artificer Rank.

* **Overtime.** When you take the Craft downtime activity, you may make an additional Craft check to add progress to another one of your crafting projects. You may not make multiple Craft checks for the same crafting project within the same downtime activity.

* **Reallocation.** When you make a Craft check, you may expend a Resource to replace the d20 outcome with a 20\.

# **Unified Model**

	*You've unlocked the arcanum, exposing the smallest packet of magical energy. All the terms of the equation are now at your disposal.*

	At 3rd Rank, after regaining expended Spell Points at the end of a long rest, you may expend a number of Spell Points up to your Artificer Rank to assign them as if they were IP. At the end of your next long rest, all IP assigned by expending Spell Points are unassigned, you immediately regain the Spell Points, and you may immediately assign them again using **Unified Model**.

# **Magical Appraisal**

	*The eye of the crafter is rarely wrong.*

At 3rd Rank, you gain the following benefits:

* **Sight.** You may cast **Keepsake's Calling**, and you may do so without needing to hold a spellcasting focus. As part of casting **Keepsake's Calling**, you may take the Sense Magic action as a free action before choosing a target object.

* **Source.** When you become aware of persistent magic through the Sense Magic action, if the persistent magic is on a spell list, you learn what the spell is regardless of which spell list it is on.

# **Ability Improvement**

	At 3rd, 6th, 9th, and 12th Rank, you gain an Ability Improvement. Each time you gain an Ability Improvement, distribute three permanent \+1 increases to your ability modifiers. You may increase the same ability modifier multiple times.

The maximum ability modifier that may be achieved is limited by your character's Level. The maximum is \+5 at Level 3, increases to \+6 at Level 6, and finally becomes \+7 at Level 9\.

# **Exploration**

	At 4th, 7th, and 10th Rank, you gain an Exploration with Artificer being the exploring Class. Each time you gain an Exploration, you gain the following benefit:

* **Bonus Rank.** You choose any Class other than Artificer and gain one Rank in the chosen Class.

# **Artificer Field Feature**

	At 4th Rank, you gain a feature for your Artificer Field.

# **Wondrous Workshop**

*Bigger on the inside, it's known as the Commercial, Resource, and Innovation Businessfront.*

At 5th Rank, as part of taking the Craft downtime activity, you may open a door to a magical demiplane that serves as your workshop. The door closes and disappears when you are done with the downtime activity. While inside, you gain the following benefits:

* **Flexible Foundry.** You may ignore the **Specialization** requirement when starting a crafting project. Craft checks you make towards a project started in this way add half your Tier Bonus instead of your full Tier Bonus.

* **Interplanar Peddler.** Instead of adding progress to a crafting project, you may instead make a Craft check against a DC of 20\. On a success, you acquire a Resource.

* **Secure Storage.** Crafting projects are held securely inside the **Wondrous Workshop** while it is no longer summoned.

# **Improvise II**

	*A violent fizzling of magical power is followed by an amorphous spark of a spell dancing between your fingertips, ready to become something new.*

	At 6th Rank, gadgets you create with **Improvise** may now be Uncommon.

# **Spell Widgets I**

*As a spell application developer, you need some good quality assurance testers.*

	At 6th Rank, at the end of a long rest, you may create 3 consumable items called *spell widgets*. For each spell widget, you choose a spell that you are able to prepare for the Artificer class to become its *stored spell,* and you must expend Spell Points equal to the stored spell's baseline Circle. You do not need to prepare any of the stored spells and you may choose the same spell more than once.

A creature holding a spell widget may cast the stored spell as a Use Item action at the spell's baseline Circle using your spellcasting modifier. Unused spell widgets lose their stored spell at the end of the next long rest.

# **Ability Improvement**

	At 6th Rank, you gain an Ability Improvement. Each time you gain an Ability Improvement, distribute three permanent \+1 increases to your ability modifiers. You may increase the same ability modifier multiple times.

The maximum ability modifier that may be achieved is limited by your character's Level. The maximum is \+5 at Level 3, increases to \+6 at Level 6, and finally becomes \+7 at Level 9\.

# **Share Results I**

	*You really feel like your exact perspective could be helpful right now.*

	At 7th Rank, as a reaction when an ally within 15 feet of you makes an INT or WIS combat check, you may add your INT to the total.

# **Exploration**

	At 7th Rank, you gain an Exploration with Artificer being the exploring Class. Each time you gain an Exploration, you gain the following benefit:

* **Bonus Rank.** You choose any Class other than Artificer and gain one Rank in the chosen Class.

# **Fine-Tuned**

	*Do you mind if Iyes, I know this wand makes flowers grow. What really is the difference between that and a fireball anyway?*

At 8th Rank, you are able to take the *Fine-Tune* downtime activity to make a permanent modification to some of your party's Curios in one of the following ways:

* **Equivalent.** You may choose a reforge trait on a Curio in your party's possession that has multiple variants, such as the **Energized** trait, and change the variant of the trait to one of your choice.

* **Exchange.** You swap the aspects of two Curios in your party's possession.

# **Artificer Field Feature**

	At 8th Rank, you gain a feature for your Artificer Field.

# **Spell Widgets II**

*Efficient power throughput for essential daily use\!*

	At 9th Rank, you no longer expend a Spell Point when storing a 1st Circle spell on a spell widget.

# **Ability Improvement**

	At 9th Rank, you gain an Ability Improvement. Each time you gain an Ability Improvement, distribute three permanent \+1 increases to your ability modifiers. You may increase the same ability modifier multiple times.

The maximum ability modifier that may be achieved is limited by your character's Level. The maximum is \+5 at Level 3, increases to \+6 at Level 6, and finally becomes \+7 at Level 9\.

# **Magical Reserves**

*Picking which item to sacrifice is difficult, as each creation is unique and beautiful in its own way.*

	At 10th Rank, once per long rest as a free action, you may choose to unassign a number of IP to produce one of the following effects:

* **Chain Reaction.** You unassign 5 IP and designate an item that was either created or modified by a feature that assigned IP. You deal your overload die as your choice of **elemental damage** to all subjects in a 10 foot radius around you or the item. You may activate this from any distance as long as you can **perceive** the item. If the item is a consumable, it is destroyed.

* **My Work for My Life.** When you are about to burn a Resolve, you may instead unassign 10 IP to gain the effects of burning a Resolve.

# **Exploration**

	At 10th Rank, you gain an Exploration with Artificer being the exploring Class. Each time you gain an Exploration, you gain the following benefit:

* **Bonus Rank.** You choose any Class other than Artificer and gain one Rank in the chosen Class.

# **Improvise III**

	*Did you know the potions you drink every day contain the same chemicals used in bombs?\!*

	At 11th Rank, gadgets you create with **Improvise** may now be Rare.

# **Share Results II**

*Your advice was so clever last time, and clearly they need more of it.*

	At 11th Rank, you may target allies up to 30 feet away with **Share Results**.

# **Maximized Arcanodynamic Potential**

*The potency of spells has been bounded by the quantity of magic fueling it. It is therefore important to research self-generative magitechnology capable of producing greater outputs than inputs into the system.*

	At 12th Rank, when you take the Craft downtime activity, you may spend any number of Resources and complete an equal number of crafting projects.

Additionally, your spell widgets no longer cost Spell Points to store the spell.

# **Ability Improvement**

	At 12th Rank, you gain an Ability Improvement. Each time you gain an Ability Improvement, distribute three permanent \+1 increases to your ability modifiers. You may increase the same ability modifier multiple times.

The maximum ability modifier that may be achieved is limited by your character's Level. The maximum is \+5 at Level 3, increases to \+6 at Level 6, and finally becomes \+7 at Level 9\.

# **Final Reach**

	When you reach character Level 12, you compute your *Total Rank*, which is calculated as:

###### The sum of all of your Class Ranks

	If your Total Rank is less than 16 when you reach Level 12, then for each deficient Rank, you may choose one of the benefits below to modify a feature granted by one of your Class Ranks:

* **Maneuvers.** You learn two additional maneuvers.

* **Battlecasting.** You gain a maneuver, an additional Spell Point, and an additional prepared spell for that Class.

* **Spellcasting.** You gain three additional Spell Points and three additional prepared spells for that Class.

# **Artificer Field: Chemistry**

	*Civilization's chief science concerns the classification, distillation, and production of the vital forces instrumental in mortal kind's survival. Whether it be the capture of fire to stave off the night, the purification of water to slake thirst, or the digging of the foundation for society, the elements persist and can be tamed.*

## **Potioncraft**

*Water, blood, and bile. Our body brews its potions, now we improve the mechanism.*

	At 1st Rank, you specialize in potion and remedy consumables and gain the following benefits:

* You learn a stock recipe for a Common potion or remedy Curio.

* When creating items via stock recipes, you may choose an extra stock recipe for a potion or remedy and create its corresponding items.

* You may add the Crafting (Alchemist) specialization to your Adventuring Trade (this does not count towards the number of specializations on that trade).

* Your Tier Bonus is doubled for Craft checks made to craft potion and remedy Curios.

## **Reagent Reserves**

*Nature always intends and strives towards the perfection of gold, but many accidents come between the change of metals.*

At 1st Rank, you have a certain number of *reagents* in your *reserves* that you use to create potions and remedies. At the end of each long rest, all unused reagents are lost and your reserves are restocked by rolling your *reagent pool,* which is a number of d4s equal to twice your Artificer Rank. Each natural value in the dice pool's outcome grants an associated reagent as shown in the table below:

## **Synthesis**

After rolling your reagent pool, you may assign one or more IP to specific reagents to add an equal number of reagents in any combination to your reserves. You may only unassign IP assigned to unused reagents.

## **Catalyzed Stock Recipe**

*Each reaction has its own unique chemical pathway, twisting about guarded secrets and around energetic hurdles. Your stockpile of potions is undoubtedly your own.*

At 1st Rank, you may assign your IP to a *catalyzed stock recipe*, which is added to your list of known stock recipes until you unassign the IP, which also renders any items created by the stock recipe inert. You may assign IP to catalyzed stock recipes multiple times, choosing a unique Curio each time from the Curio Compendium. You must assign 1/2/3/4 IP for a Common/Uncommon/Rare/Legendary item, and you are able to do so at 1st/4th/7th/10th Rank, respectively.

Each time you use a catalyzed stock recipe, you may either create its chosen potion or remedy as its *catalyzed brew* or a general *craft brew* (details further below).

### *Catalyzed Brew*

Each time you create a catalyzed brew, you must additionally consume certain reagents from your reserves, following these Laws of Alchemy:

* **Law of Constant Composition.** You must consume at least 1 reagent for each IP assigned to the recipe.

* **Law of Equivalent Exchange.** If not determined by the following Laws, any reagent may be used to satisfy the **Law of Constant Composition**.

* **Law of Calcination.** You must consume a **fire** (![][image6]) reagent if the Curio improves offensive combat abilities (e.g. attacks, dealing damage, critical hits, applying conditions).

* **Law of Dissolution.** You must consume a **water** (![][image7]) reagent if the Curio causes the user to regain ST or heal HP.

* **Law of Separation.** You must consume an **air** (![][image8]) reagent if the Curio cleanses conditions, increases Speed, or grants mobilities.

* **Law of Conjunction.** You must consume an **earth** (![][image9]) reagent if the Curio improves defensive combat abilities (e.g. damage buffers, saving throw benefits, resisting conditions).

### *Craft Brew*

	Each time you create a craft brew, you may simply combine reagents, following the **Law of Constant Composition**, to produce two potions that each grant the following benefits:

* **Fire.** Once per round until the end of their next short rest, when the drinker hits with an attack, they may add your overload die to the damage roll. Each extra ![][image6] reagent consumed after the first adds an additional \+5 bonus to this damage roll.

* **Air.** Until the end of their next short rest, the drinker gains \+10 feet Speed for each ![][image8] reagent consumed.

* **Water.** The drinker regains 10 ST and 5 HP for each ![][image7] reagent consumed.

* **Earth.** Until the end of their next short rest, the drinker gains a DB 5 for each ![][image9] reagent consumed against a single damage type.

If more than one of the same reagent is used for a craft brew, the effects stack. Potion effects created by your craft brews that last until the end of a next short rest, regardless of composition, all have the same Toxicity effect:

* **Experimental.** While suffering from potion toxicity, you are poisoned.

## **Tria Prima**

*All of the seven metals are born from a threefold matter. Mercury is the spirit, sulphur is the soul, and salt is the body. The soul unites those two contraries, the body and spirit, and changes them into one essence.*

At 4th Rank, your potions gain extra benefits when you combine reagents in specific ways. If a pair of elements in the set you consumed to make a **Catalyzed Stock Recipe** is listed below, you may choose to append its saving throw bonuses to the benefits of the potion or remedy you create.

* **Sulphur.** If ![][image6] and ![][image8] reagents are both present, the drinker additionally gains advantage on all **Will** and **Courage** saving throws until the end of the next short or long rest.

* **Mercury.** If ![][image8] and ![][image7] reagents are both present, the drinker additionally gains advantage on all **Reflex** and **Quick-Wits** saving throws until the end of the next short or long rest.

* **Salt.** If ![][image7] and ![][image9] reagents are both present, the drinker additionally gains advantage on all **Poise** and **Fortitude** saving throws until the end of the next short or long rest.

## **Transmutation**

	*The goal of the ancients was to change lead to gold. I'll settle for a healing potion.*

At 4th Rank, you may transmute the reagent dice pool before rolling it. Assign 1 IP to this feature and choose a reagent. Any natural values rolled that would give you the chosen reagent are instead replaced with other reagents of your choice.

## **Advanced Lab Techniques**

*True mastery does not arise from the novelty of formulae but in the efficiency and efficacy of the process.*

At 8th Rank, you gain the following improvements.

* **Fermentation.** You may replace the toxicity effect on potions created before your last long rest with the following:

  * **Alcoholic.** You gain disadvantage on **Perceive** checks.

* **Distillation.** Immediately before creating items with stock recipes, you may unassign IP from a Catalyzed Stock Recipe of your choice, rendering any items created by it since your last long rest inert. Doing so refunds the reagents used to make them.

* **Coagulation.** You may take three actions to create a single item from one of your Catalyzed Stock Recipes using your reagents.

# **Artificer Field: Engraving**

	*Your craftwork is the art of metal, of smoke, of calligraphy, and most of all, of persistence. Hours have been poured into the exact heft of the handle, the honed sharpness of its edge, and the intricacies of the fizzling runes lining the prototype. Words have power, lending their weight to the blow.*

## **Runecraft**

*Blood, sweat, and tears temper the steel of your work. Your dreams are alloyed with your plans, and they begin to take shape.*

At 1st Rank, you specialize in weapon equipment and gain the following benefits:

* You acquire a Common weapon Curio purposed for melee fighting styles.

* You may add the Crafting (Battlesmith) specialization to your Adventuring Trade (this does not count towards the number of specializations on that trade).

* Your Tier Bonus is doubled for Craft checks made to craft weapons purposed for melee fighting styles and upgrades for them.

## **Energized Runes**

	*The last careful etchings into the steel provide the final touches required to ignite the blade with power.*

At 1st Rank, at the end of each of your long rests, you may assign your IP to weapons purposed for melee fighting styles to append additional reforge traits called *engraver traits* to the *engraved weapon* (the List of Engraver Traits is described further below). You may assign 1 or 3 IP to each engraver trait.

	When you make a melee weapon attack with an engraved weapon, you may *invoke* one of the engraver traits and convert all damage dealt by the attack to the damage type of the trait, adding any additional effects as listed in its description.

Once per round when you hit with an attack for which you invoked an engraver trait, you may also add your overload die to the damage dealt by the attack.

## **Intensified Inlays**

	*As pronunciation matters to chanted magic, so the diacritics hone the inscribed.*

	At 4th Rank, you may now assign 1, 3, 5, or 7 IP to an engraver trait.

## **Runic Overdrive**

	*In a fantastic upgrade, you line the weapon with power channels able to redirect and concentrate its energies at the convenience of an array of tenuously connected knobs and dials.*

At 8th Rank, as a free action, you may unassign the IP of one of your engraver traits to regain the usage of the aspect of the engraved weapon. If you do so immediately before using the aspect, you may add the engraver trait's listed *aspect booster* to the aspect's effect.

# **Artificer Field: Wandcarving**

	*Oak for its sturdiness. Maple for its reliability. Willow for its whimsy. Regardless of the choice, the grain of possibility runs along its length. The scraps that curl off of the board as it is planed down to size smell of the deep magic in the forest of its birth. Even the sawdust from its turning maintains a hint of enchantment.*

## **Wandcraft**

*The spellcaster's stick, the legerdemain's ledger; whatever its shape, you can make it.*

At 1st Rank, you specialize in spellcasting focus equipment and gain the following benefits:

* You acquire a Common spellcasting focus Curio.

* You may add the Crafting (Scrivener) specialization to your Adventuring Trade (this does not count towards the number of specializations on that trade).

* Your Tier Bonus is doubled for Craft checks made to craft spellcasting foci and upgrades for them.

## **Chaser Bolt**

	*Mundane wands absorb the shock of sorcery, but when magic already permeates every pore of the wood through which spells are channeled, the addition of a safety release valve is necessary. My safety, not theirs.*

At 1st Rank, once per rest when you cast a spell, you may add your overload die to the spell's damage roll.

## **Expanded Imbuement**

	*The smallest packet of quantized magic easily finds its home in existing arcanergetic infrastructure.*

At 1st Rank, at the end of each of your long rests, you may assign your IP to one or more spellcasting focus's **Imbued Spells** aspect to append additional Arcane spells to its spell list. Each spell you append to the list requires assigning IP equal to its baseline Circle and adds the same number of Spell Charges to the aspect.

The baseline Circle of an appended spell has the following requirements:

* **Bounded.** The appended spell's Circle cannot be higher than the spell with the highest baseline Circle in the aspect's spell list.
* **Preparable.** The appended spell's Circle must be of a Circle you are capable of preparing for the Artificer Class.

	You may heighten spells you cast using the Imbued Spells aspect up to the highest baseline Circle in the aspect's spell list.

## **Weaponized Wands**

	*Wands slung low flick in and out of holsters with dizzying speed, peppering the enemy with magical munitions.*

At 4th Rank, spellcasting foci now count for you as weapons purposed for the Basic Ranged fighting style without the **Ammunition** trait.

Whenever you cast a cantrip that makes a ranged spell attack, you may *weaponize* it, granting it the following benefits:

* **Attack Type.** The weaponized cantrip attack is converted from a ranged spell attack to a ranged weapon attack using the Basic Ranged fighting style.

* **Damage Roll.** The weaponized cantrip spell damage roll is replaced by a weapon attack damage roll, as calculated below:

Your Martial Die \+ your DEX \+ your Tier Bonus

* **Damage Type.** The type of damage dealt by the spellcasting focus is the same as that of the weaponized cantrip's spell damage roll.

* **No Discharge Trait.** If the cantrip has the discharge trait (as noted in its description), you may not use it.

Additionally, whenever you use a spellcasting focus to make a weapon attack with the Basic Ranged fighting style, you may instead cast a weaponized cantrip (using any feature you have) as above.

## **Elaborate Lathework**

	*The mother of magical machines.*

At 8th Rank, your spellcasting focus Imbued Aspects are improved in the following ways:

* **Reload.** When you score a critical hit with a weaponized cantrip attack, your Imbued Spells aspect regains 1 Spell Charge.

* **High Caliber Imbuement.** When you append spells to an Imbued Spells aspect using the **Expanded Imbuement** feature, you may ignore the **Preparable** requirement for a single spell.
