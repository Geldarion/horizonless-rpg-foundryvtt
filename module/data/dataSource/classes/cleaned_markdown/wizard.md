# Arcane Spellcasting

As a Wizard, you are able to prepare and cast spells from the Arcane
spell list:

- **Spells Prepared.** At 1st Rank, you may prepare 6 spells for this
  Class at the end of each long rest. You may cast those prepared spells
  using this feature.

- **Class Circle Cap.** Each spell you prepare may have a maximum
  baseline Circle of 1st Circle.

- **Heightening.** When you cast spells using this feature, you may
  heighten spells up to your Class Circle Cap.

- **Spell Points.** At 1st Rank, you gain 4 Spell Points. When you cast
  spells using this feature, you must expend Spell Points equal to the
  Circle of the spell. Spell Points are regained at the end of each long
  rest.

- **Spellcasting Modifier.** Your spellcasting modifier when casting
  spells using this feature is INT.

  - **Your Spell Attack Bonus** = your INT + your Tier Bonus

  - **Your Spell Save DC** = 8 + your INT + your Tier Bonus

  - **Spellcasting Checks** made with spells prepared for this class use
    your INT.

- **Signature Spell.** Your designated signature spell may be one of the
  1st Circle or higher spells prepared for this feature.

- **Higher Ranks.** At higher Ranks, the spells prepared, Spell Points,
  and Class Circle Cap all improve as described in the Class Overview
  Table.

# Wizard Study

*Lifetimes of study could be spent on just a single topic. The more you
learn, the more you find how much there is you don't know.*

At 1st Rank, you choose a Wizard Study from which you develop your
magical treatises and practices. You gain features from your chosen
Wizard Study at 1st, 4th, and 8th Rank.

# Spell Theorems

*Recursive runes and algebraic abracadabrum mark the scrolls held by the
learned mage.*

At 1st Rank, as a free action when you cast a spell, you may apply the
effect of one of your known *spell theorems* to the spell, modifying its
properties and behaviors.

Each spell theorem requires an associated number of *Theorem Points* to
be expended for it to take effect. At 1st Rank, you have a pool of 3
Theorem Points to spend on spell theorems to modify your spells, and you
gain more as your Wizard Rank increases.

At the end of a long rest, your remaining pool of Theorem Points resets
to an amount corresponding to your Wizard Rank, as described in the
Class Overview Table.

## Spell Theorems Known

At 1st Rank, you learn the two *general spell theorems* described below,
as well as one spell theorem from your Wizard Study.

### Continuity Theorem

Wizard Spell Theorem

When you cast a spell, you may spend 1 Theorem Point to double the
spell's duration. Spells whose durations are not determinable at the
casting of the spell, such as spells that persist until you complete a
long rest, are not affected by this spell theorem.

### Dislocation Theorem

Wizard Spell Theorem

When you cast a spell, you may spend 1 Theorem Point to double the range
of the spell. If the spell's range is Reach, its range is extended to 15
feet.

# Spellbook

*Knowledge is power, and powerful knowledge indeed is held within the
infinite pages of this daedal directory. Rather than being written or
printed, spellbooks are repositories for your knowledge and secrets and
can come in many forms.*

At 2nd Rank, as three actions once per long rest, you may choose an
Arcane spell you are able to prepare for the Wizard class and append it
to the list of spells you have prepared for the Wizard class. Spells
prepared by your **Spellbook** feature remain so until the end of your
next long rest.

# Spell Scrawls I

*Spells created by your colleagues are sometimes serviceable, but the
peer review process is crucial for reproducibility in new contexts.*

At 2nd Rank, select one of the following Spell Scrawl features from the
list below, which allow you to modify the spells you cast. During the
Retrain downtime activity, you may redo the selection of all of your
chosen Spellbook Scrawls.

You may take an additional action to *scrawl* when an Arcane spell
effect you create begins. When you scrawl, you may apply one or more of
your chosen Spell Scrawl features to the spell effect.

### Acerbic Comments

Wizard Spellbook Scrawl

You change the spell effect description to substitute any saving throw
with a **Reflex** saving throw or any damage type with **acid**.

### Blunt Opinions

Wizard Spellbook Scrawl

You change the spell effect description to substitute any saving throw
with a **Poise** saving throw or any damage type with **bludgeoning**.

### Cold Analysis

Wizard Spellbook Scrawl

You change the spell effect description to substitute any saving throw
with a **Fortitude** saving throw or any damage type with **cold**.

### Forced Politeness

Wizard Spellbook Scrawl

You change the spell effect description to substitute any saving throw
with a **Will** saving throw or any damage type with **force**.

### Scathing Criticism

Wizard Spellbook Scrawl

You change the spell effect description to substitute any saving throw
with a **Courage** saving throw or any damage type with **fire**.

### Sharp Observations

Wizard Spellbook Scrawl

You change the spell effect description to substitute any saving throw
with a **Quick-Wits** saving throw or any damage type with
**lightning**.

# Theory of Redirection

*You learn to ricochet your sorcery to recover a bit of professional
pride.*

At 3rd Rank, you learn the following general spell theorem:

### Redirection Theorem

3rd Rank Wizard Spell Theorem

When you miss with a spell attack, you may spend 1 Theorem Point to
cause the spell attack to switch to a new target within range and retry
it.

# Ability Improvement

At 3rd, 6th, and 9th Rank, you gain an Ability Improvement. Each time
you gain an Ability Improvement, distribute three permanent +1 increases
to your ability modifiers. You may increase the same ability modifier
multiple times.

The maximum ability modifier that may be achieved is limited by your
character's Level. The maximum is +5 at Level 3, increases to +6 at
Level 6, and finally becomes +7 at Level 9.

# Exploration

At 4th, 7th, and 10th Rank, you gain an Exploration with Wizard being
the exploring Class. Each time you gain an Exploration, you gain the
following benefit:

- **Bonus Rank.** You choose any Class other than Wizard and gain one
  Rank in the chosen Class.

# Wizard Study Feature

At 4th Rank, you gain a feature for your Wizard Study.

# Quick Scrawl

*To use this spell in this circumstance would be an obvious error for
most people, but you are not most people.*

At 5th Rank, once per rest when you use your **Spell Scrawls** feature,
you may scrawl as a free action.

# Contingency Spells

*Do not subvert the plans of wizards, for even you are a factor in the
arcane calculus.*

At 6th Rank, at the end of each long rest, you may choose one of the
spells from the Arcane spell list and create a *contingency*. A
contingency contains a condition of your design set at the time of
creation that, when fulfilled, immediately casts the spell at the
highest Circle you are able to prepare for the Wizard class with a
predetermined target. The contingency persists until either it takes
effect or you use this feature again.

Below are some examples of contingencies you are able to create, but are
not limited to:

- When you become stuck, cast **Translocate** to escape,
  targeting an unoccupied space within range.

- When you fall farther than 20 feet, cast **Hover** targeting yourself.

- When you die, cast **Fireball** targeting your space.

Spells cast using contingencies do not cost Spell Points and none of its
spell traits may be activated. You cannot apply Spell Theorems to spells
cast using contingencies.

Your contingencies cannot be triggered by anything you have caused
yourself and they cannot be trivially triggered, such as speaking a
command word or anything else that involves a free action.

# Theory of Potentiation

*Each spellcasting individual's experiences, wisdom, and personality
brings a different lens through which to view magic. You, however, have
thoroughly-tested models and thus no need for such things.*

At 6th Rank, you learn the following general spell theorem:

### Potentiation Theorem

*6th Rank Wizard Spell Theorem*

When you heighten a spell and are provided an enhancement option that
requires a particular ability modifier, you may expend 2 Theorem Points
to ignore that requirement.

# Ability Improvement

At 6th Rank, you gain an Ability Improvement. Each time you gain an
Ability Improvement, distribute three permanent +1 increases to your
ability modifiers. You may increase the same ability modifier multiple
times.

The maximum ability modifier that may be achieved is limited by your
character's Level. The maximum is +5 at Level 3, increases to +6 at
Level 6, and finally becomes +7 at Level 9.

# Exploration

At 7th Rank, you gain an Exploration with Wizard being the exploring
Class. Each time you gain an Exploration, you gain the following
benefit:

- **Bonus Rank.** You choose any Class other than Wizard and gain one
  Rank in the chosen Class.

# Spell Scrawls II

*A meta-analysis of the current spell research shows that your
colleagues, unfortunately, continue to miss their work's true
potential.*

At 8th Rank, select an additional Spell Scrawl feature chosen from those
listed at 2nd Rank. During the Retrain downtime activity, you may redo
the selection of all of your chosen Spell Scrawls.

Additionally, when you scrawl, you may change the spell effect
description to substitute both the saving throw and the damage type.

# Wizard Study Feature

At 8th Rank, you gain a feature for your Wizard Study .

# Theory of Crystallization

*Maximize your productivity through delegation and automation. Work
smarter, not harder!*

At 9th Rank, You learn the following general spell theorem:

### Crystallization Theorem

*9th Rank Wizard Spell Theorem*

When you cast a spell that requires concentration, you may expend 3
Theorem Points to create a *concentration crystal* at a target
unoccupied space within 5 feet of you, which is a Small automaton object
that concentrates on the spell for you.

The concentration crystal has 45 HP, 19 AC, 0 Toughness, immunity to
**physical damage**, a Speed equal to your own, and advanced Hovering
mobility. When you move, it moves with you, staying within 5 feet of you
at all times. The spell lasts its normal duration or until the
concentration crystal is destroyed, whichever happens first.

# Ability Improvement

At 9th Rank, you gain an Ability Improvement. Each time you gain an
Ability Improvement, distribute three permanent +1 increases to your
ability modifiers. You may increase the same ability modifier multiple
times.

The maximum ability modifier that may be achieved is limited by your
character's Level. The maximum is +5 at Level 3, increases to +6 at
Level 6, and finally becomes +7 at Level 9.

# Second Contingency

"*Plan A is for everything to go according to plan. Plans B--Z are in
place, but I die in Plans M--P, and the rest of the party dies in Plans
T, U, and X, so let's stick to the plan, shall we?"*

At 10th Rank, you may now use **Contingency** **Spells** multiple times
at the end of a long rest to create up to two contingencies that are
both active at once. Creating a third contingency requires ending a
previous one of your choice. Each contingency may have their own unique
condition and spell that it casts.

# Exploration

At 10th Rank, you gain an Exploration with Wizard being the exploring
Class. Each time you gain an Exploration, you gain the following
benefit:

- **Bonus Rank.** You choose any Class other than Wizard and gain one
  Rank in the chosen Class.

# Generalized Scrawl

*Your mastery of the arcane clearly demonstrated by your comprehensive
compendium of improvements to the works of those who came before you.
They might as well be your spells now.*

At 11th Rank, you may now also scrawl onto any non-Arcane spell effects
you create.

# The Fundamental Theory of Arcane Actualization

*You have solved the last equation needed to complete the set of
universal laws of magic. Your mastery knows no bounds as you unlock the
secrets of arithmancy.*

At 12th Rank, you learn the following general spell theorem:

### Fundamental Theorem

*12th Rank Wizard Spell Theorem*

When you cast a spell, you may expend 4 Theorem Points to reduce the
casting time by 1 action.

# Final Reach

When you reach character Level 12, you compute your *Total Rank*, which
is calculated as:

###### The sum of all of your Class Ranks

If your Total Rank is less than 16 when you reach Level 12, then for
each deficient Rank, you may choose one of the benefits below to modify
a feature granted by one of your Class Ranks:

- **Maneuvers.** You learn two additional maneuvers.

- **Battlecasting.** You gain a maneuver, an additional Spell Point, and
  an additional prepared spell for that Class.

- **Spellcasting.** You gain three additional Spell Points and three
  additional prepared spells for that Class.

# Wizard Study: Dynamancy

*Peering through the surface of the waters casts a line of vision into
the infinite elegancies of the motions of the universe. All in equal
balance, each action provokes an equivalent reaction. As droplets of
magic plunge into the lake of knowledge, overlapping concentric rings
ripple out from their seemingly chaotic introduction. No matter the
rain, eternal truths and geometries will continue to hold.*

## Adsorption Sigil

*Great arcs split off from a central spiral and branch into a myriad of
precise fractal structures. Each turning of the line of motion produces
an uncountable multitude of deterministic consequences.*

At 1st Rank, as an action, you may grant your *adsorption sigil* to a
target creature within 30 feet of you that persists until it is expended
or you grant another adsorption sigil. A creature with your adsorption
sigil gains the following benefits:

- **Damage Buffer.** They gain DB 5 against **elemental damage**. This
  DB becomes 10/15/20 at 4th/7th/10th Rank, respectively.

- **Dynamo Dice.** They start with a pool of 0d6 dynamo dice. Each time
  the adsorption sigil's DB is used to reduce damage taken, they may add
  1d6 to the dynamo dice pool and store the dealt damage type onto the
  adsorption sigil. All dynamo dice in the pool are discarded at the
  start and end of combat.

- **Infused Strike.** When they hit with an attack, they may expend the
  adsorption sigil to add all of the dynamo dice in the pool to the
  damage roll. If they do, all of the damage dealt is converted to the
  most recently stored damage type on the adsorption sigil.

## Theory of Duplication

*When approaching the near-equilibrium state, the maximum dynamic rate
of power redirected through adsorption can be represented as*
$\left( r_{output} - r_{input} \right) \approx 7 \cdot 2^{R} - \frac{20}{3}(R + 2)$*,
where R is the relative power of the sigil's creator.*

At 1st Rank, you learn the **Duplication Theorem** spell theorem
described below:

### Duplication Theorem

Dynamancy Wizard Spell Theorem

When you cast a spell that targets the creature with your adsorption
sigil, you may expend 2 Theorem Points to double the number of dynamo
dice in the pool. An adsorption sigil's dynamo dice pool cannot gain the
benefits of **Duplication Theorem** more than once.

## Inscribed Abjuration

*Endless symmetries align and reform into a concentrated vector.*

At 4th Rank, whenever you scrawl, you may also use your **Adsorption
Sigil** feature as a free action.

## Reflective Geometry

*Your mind is opened to higher-dimensional symmetries. Your spellwork
easily projects the magic existing in disparate orthogonal planes onto
the axis of your reality.*

At 8th Rank, you gain the following benefits:

- **Faceted Shielding.** The DB from your adsorption sigil may now also
  be used against **supernatural** damage types.

- **Force Multiplier.** Immediately before the creature with your
  adsorption sigil makes an attack, they may expend one or more dynamo
  dice from the pool to reduce the attack's critical hit threshold by
  the same amount as the number of dice expended.

# Wizard Study: Entropomancy

*The sand pours through the hourglass, the ink dries on the page, and
the smoke of a recent explosion dissipates slowly. Time moves ever on,
draining the life from each creature and creating new life to consume
more heat. Some look through the screen from the other side, watching as
shattered glass reforms into the vase that was disturbed. Others
accelerate the shattered glass's dispersion, grinding it into sand that
pours...*

### Automaton Spells

Various features below refer to *automaton spells*, which are spells
with the **Automaton** trait.

## Nonspontaneous Order

*You reverse time's arrow and restore the decay of age.*

At 1st Rank, you may append the **Mending** cantrip to the list of
spells you have prepared for the Wizard class. Your **Mending** spell
gains the following improvements:

- **Automata Reanimation.** When an automaton object from one of your
  automaton spells is destroyed, you may allow the spell effect to
  continue and deposit some inanimate *remains* at its location that
  persist until the spell ends. You may cast **Mending** on the remains
  regardless of their Size to return the remains to being the automaton
  object again with its HP fully healed.

- **Improved Threshold.** The maximum Toughness of an object you may
  target with the spell becomes 5 × your Tier Bonus.

## Theory of Deceleration

*The deceleration factor approaches five-eights of the Arcanum spent to
the third power, accounting for the subtraction of the exponential decay
with respect to the same Arcanum. Plus one, for some reason. The
derivation is left as an exercise to the apprentice, QED:*

$\frac{d^{2}A}{dt^{2}} =$$\frac{5}{8}A^{3} - e^{A} + 1$*.*

At 1st Rank, you learn the **Deceleration Theorem** spell theorem
described below:

### Deceleration Theorem

Entropomancy Wizard Spell Theorem

When you cast an automaton spell, you may expend 1 Theorem Point. If you
do, when the automaton object is destroyed, you may cause all creatures
and objects within 15 feet of the object to become slowed
with the **Recoverable** condition trait, allowing an action to end it.

## Spontaneous Decay

*The universe and all its creation trends towards smaller and more
insignificant dust. Why should magic be any different?*

At 4th Rank, when you cast an automaton spell as a prepared spell, you
may choose to consume the remains generated from your **Nonspontaneous
Order** feature if the new automaton spell is at a lower Circle than the
original automaton spell. If you do, the Spell Point cost is reduced by
one (to a minimum of 0).

## Absolute Zero

*Equilibrium, ultimately, is the pursuit of equality and oneness. If
only one state remains, it must be perfect in its stillness.*

At 8th Rank, you gain the following benefits:

- **Perfect Crystal.** While a subject is affected by **Deceleration
  Theorem**, instead of halving their Speed, you may cause them to make
  a **Fortitude** saving throw against your Wizard Spell Save DC to
  avoid becoming stuck with the **Recoverable** condition
  trait. Impact rolls made by creatures stuck in this way
  have the outcomes of all dice rolled replaced by 1.

- **Spatial Delocalization.** When you cast **Mending**, you may cause
  the spell's range to become 30 feet.

# Wizard Study: Magnamancy

*Frozen crystals expand and overtake, minute embers stoke into raucous
inferno, and fibrous planar energies compress into interstellar blades.
As the ultimate expression of arcane mastery, your theory hones in on
the intensification of magic. The truest measure is output and your
spellwork bears witness to this fact.*

## Sculpting Glyph

*Through a masterful externalization of the rune circle, you can shape
the spell to perfectly match its surroundings.*

At 1st Rank, as a reaction at any point, you may place a *sculpting
glyph* on a target within 60 feet of you that persists for 10 minutes.
You must concentrate on this effect similar to if it were a spell
effect. When a subject with your sculpting glyph would be affected by
one of your spell effects, you may cause them to instead be unaffected
by it.

## Theory of Amplitude

*While the real-quantification of a spell's potential is typically
expressed as* $C$*, this is a shorthand for the expanded form of*
$\sum_{k = 0}^{\infty}C \cdot r^{k} = C \cdot \frac{1}{1 - \ r}$*, where
r is the reverberant attenuation factor (a value often nearly-equal to 1
when amplifier glyphs are not applied to the spell).*

At 1st Rank, you learn the **Amplitude Theorem** spell theorem described
below:

### Amplitude Theorem

Magnamancy Wizard Spell Theorem

When you cast a 1st Circle or higher spell, you may expend Theorem
Points equal to the Circle of the spell. If you do, all dice for the
spell effect's damage rolls *explode* if the outcome is its maximum
value (e.g. roll a 6 on a d6 causes it to explode). Dice that explode
add another die of its size to the damage roll, and those dice may also
explode if their outcome is their maximum value.

## Disastrous Spectacle

*A carefully-laid display of power is never without an encore.*

At 4th Rank, when you scrawl on a spell that deals damage, each time a
creature is killed, knocked out, or burns a Resolve from the damage
dealt by the spell effect, you may deal **5 force damage** to all other
creatures and unattended objects within 10 feet of them. Each subject
may only be dealt this damage once per spell.

This additional damage is counted as part of the spell effect and thus
can be affected by features such as **Sculpting Glyph**.

## Relentless Grandiosity

*Not a moment wasted in pursuit of the biggest explosion ever.*

At 8th Rank, you gain the following benefits:

- **Destructive Design.** When you use your **Sculpting Glyph** feature,
  you may place a sculpting glyph on up to three targets rather than
  one.

- **Recirculation.** Once per rest, when you cast your signature spell
  and apply your **Amplitude Theorem** to it, you may halve the Theorem
  Point cost.
